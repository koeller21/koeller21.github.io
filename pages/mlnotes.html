Dataset Notation

Let a dataset consist of \( N \) tuples \( (x_i, y_i)\), with \( i \in \mathbf{A} \subset \N \text{ and } \mathbf{card}(\mathbf{A}) = N \).

Let \( x_i \) be an element of \( R^D \), where \( D \) indicates the dimensionality of the training data. In image classification, this might be the image size, flattened out. E.g. if an image is \( 32 \) pixels in width and \( 32 \) pixels in height, with \( 3 \) color channels, then \( D = 32 \cdot 32 \cdot 3 = 3072 \).

Let \( y_i \) \( \in \{ 0, \ldots, K \} \subset \N \). Therefore, \( y_i \) represents the ground truth class label that \( x_i \) has been assigned with.
